# Ontology of Learning

Five levels of structure, from a single moment of understanding to a life direction. Each level nests inside the next. Teachers use this ontology to design and pace the learning experience.

## The Five Levels

### ✦ Spark

The atomic unit of learning. One idea meets one interaction — an explanation paired with an exercise, a question paired with a discovery, a concept paired with a moment of practice.

- **Duration**: 5–10 minutes (fixed, does not vary by commitment)
- **Contains**: 1 concept + 1 interaction
- **Example**: "A chord inversion flips which note is on the bottom. Try moving the C to the top — hear the difference?"

### ◉ Session

A complete conversation with a beginning, middle, and end. The natural rhythm of one sitting — long enough to build momentum, short enough to stay sharp.

- **Duration**: 30–60 minutes (fixed, does not vary by commitment)
- **Contains**: 3–6 Sparks
- **Example**: "Today: we'll hear why minor chords sound sad, build three of them, then play a progression that uses all three."

### ◈ Chapter

A group of sessions around one sub-topic. A chapter ends when the learner has unlocked a new ability — something they couldn't do before.

- **Contains**: 4–8 Sessions
- **Duration by commitment**:
  - Casual (1–2 sessions/week): 3–6 weeks
  - Regular (3–4 sessions/week): 1.5–3 weeks
  - Intensive (5–7 sessions/week): 1–1.5 weeks
- **Example**: "By the end of this chapter, you can improvise over any 12-bar blues."

### ◇ Journey

The full learning arc across multiple chapters. What others call a course — but this one is shaped by the learner, not a syllabus.

- **Contains**: 3–8 Chapters
- **Duration by commitment**:
  - Casual: 3–12 months
  - Regular: 1.5–6 months
  - Intensive: 3 weeks–3 months
- **Example**: "Your journey through music theory: from first chord to writing your own progressions."

### △ Path

Multiple journeys chained toward a bigger ambition. A life direction, not just a course — the kind of learning that changes what you're capable of.

- **Contains**: 2–5 Journeys
- **Duration by commitment**:
  - Casual: 1–3 years
  - Regular: 6–18 months
  - Intensive: 3–10 months
- **Example**: "The path to becoming a musician: theory, instrument, composition, performance."

## Composition

```
△ Path
 └─ 2–5 × ◇ Journey
     └─ 3–8 × ◈ Chapter
         └─ 4–8 × ◉ Session
             └─ 3–6 × ✦ Spark
```

## Crossings

Learning paths are not isolated tracks. Knowledge converges. A chapter on harmonics belongs to both Music Theory and Sound Engineering. Crossings are moments where a learner's understanding deepens because they see the same idea from two disciplines.

A teacher at a crossing draws from both paths, creating connections the learner couldn't make alone. The Meta-Teacher should recognize when a new teacher's domain overlaps with an existing path and create crossing points automatically.

## Commitment Levels

| Level | Sessions per week | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Casual | 1–2 | Learning at a relaxed pace, fitting sessions around life |
| Regular | 3–4 | A steady rhythm, treating learning as a recurring practice |
| Intensive | 5–7 | Deep immersion, making learning the primary focus |

Sparks and Sessions have fixed durations regardless of commitment — attention limits don't change with frequency. Chapters, Journeys, and Paths scale based on how often the learner shows up.

## Traditional Names Reference

How other traditions name these same levels:

### ✦ Spark — the atomic unit
- 🏫 K-12: Learning objective
- 🎓 University: Concept
- 🏢 Corporate L&D: Micro-learning
- 💻 Online Platforms: Bite / Card
- 🌱 Montessori: Exercise

### ◉ Session — one sitting
- 🏫 K-12: Lesson
- 🎓 University: Lecture / Seminar
- 🏢 Corporate L&D: Session
- 💻 Online Platforms: Lesson
- 🌱 Montessori: Work period

### ◈ Chapter — a new ability unlocked
- 🏫 K-12: Unit
- 🎓 University: Module
- 🏢 Corporate L&D: Module
- 💻 Online Platforms: Section
- 🌱 Montessori: Theme

### ◇ Journey — a complete learning arc
- 🏫 K-12: Course
- 🎓 University: Course
- 🏢 Corporate L&D: Program
- 💻 Online Platforms: Course
- 🌱 Montessori: Study

### △ Path — a life direction
- 🏫 K-12: Curriculum
- 🎓 University: Degree / Major
- 🏢 Corporate L&D: Learning path
- 💻 Online Platforms: Track / Specialization
- 🌱 Montessori: Cycle
